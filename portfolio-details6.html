  • Published: IEEE Xplore
  • GitHub: View Repository

The Problem

Existing admission prediction systems rely solely on numeric scores (GPA, test results), ignoring the qualitative richness of Statements of Purpose and Letters of Recommendation — which are often decisive in top-program admissions.

What I Built

  • Performed exploratory data analysis on undergraduate profiles including GPA, test scores, and extracurriculars to identify the strongest predictors of admission success
  • Trained and compared Linear, Ridge, and Lasso regression models, achieving 80% prediction accuracy
  • Designed a novel unsupervised pipeline combining K-Means clustering with NLP to evaluate and rank SOPs and LORs — boosting admission prediction precision by 25%

Impact

This work was published in IEEE Xplore, validating the approach as a meaningful contribution to data-driven education research. The NLP-augmented model significantly outperforms score-only baselines.
